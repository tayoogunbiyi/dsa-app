Week 3 & 4 Resources

  • Week 3 & 4's Topic is On Hash Tables & Hash Sets
  • There's a small subsection in CTCI about Hash tables (Pg 99)
  • Another good resource is this link
  • Hash tables are super useful and can often be a neat trick for reducing the runtime of many algos
  • They offer O(1) inserts and delete. They can also be used to find duplicates, keeping track of stuff we've seen previosly during an iteration etc
  • Good practice involves attempting to write a hash table from scratch.
  • Hash sets are similar to hash tables with the difference being that they just have values. Hash tables have key & value pairs. Sets are usually more appropriate when we don't need to keep track of a key-value mapping and we just need the value (or the key)
  • Question Of The Week - What is a collision in hash tables and how can we work around them?
